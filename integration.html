Psychedelic Integration Support

You've journeyed beyond what the eye can see—let us anchor what you experienced into the life you share.

These sessions gently support the emotional, spiritual, and existential insights gained during your psychedelic experience. We create space for the medicine or psychedelics. We make personalized meaning from the journey, and craft pathways to help weave it into real life.

For journeyers feeling cracked open, but seeking the discipline, or called for balance and to evoke what came through.

What Integration Offers

Psychedelic experiences can be profound, overwhelming, beautiful, and destabilizing all at once. Integration is the bridge between the journey and your everyday life—a space to make sense of what surfaced, to honor what was revealed, and to ground the insights into meaningful change.

Whether you're processing a recent journey or working with experiences from months or years ago, integration creates a container for:

  • Making meaning of symbolic, emotional, or spiritual content
  • Processing difficult or challenging experiences
  • Translating insights into practical life changes
  • Navigating shifts in identity, relationships, or worldview
  • Grounding expanded states back into embodied presence

How Sessions Work

Integration sessions are held as 60–90 minute conversations via video or phone. We move at your pace, honoring whatever needs to emerge.

Sessions may include:

  • Witnessing and holding space for your experience
  • Somatic practices to help ground and regulate
  • Meaning-making through metaphor, ritual, or creative reflection
  • Identifying actionable steps to embody your insights
  • Navigating relationship or identity shifts that surfaced
  • Processing fear, grief, or confusion that arose

This isn't therapy, but it is therapeutic. It's a space where your journey is welcomed without judgment, and where integration becomes a conscious, supported process.

Who This Is For

  • Anyone who has worked with plant medicines, psychedelics, or expanded states
  • People processing a recent or past journey
  • Those navigating challenging or confusing experiences
  • Anyone seeking to translate insights into real-world change
  • People experiencing shifts in identity, worldview, or relationships post-journey

Please note: I do not provide substances, facilitate journeys, or offer medical advice. This is integration support only.
